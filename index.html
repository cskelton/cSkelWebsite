About

My name is Cathal Skelton, a 26 year old Software Developer, located in Co. Tyrone, Ireland.

I was pretty late to the Software Development scene having studied History and Politics at Queen's University Belfast. Due to the job market and always having the desire to learn about developing software and creating front-end services, I began retraining myself, taking full advantage of an OCN Level 4 qualification in Software Programming, being run by the VISTA Training Academy during the summer of 2015.

After gaining the OCN qualification, I studied for and passed the ISTQB Software Tester Foundation exam and promptly gained employment with Aptcode Solutions, firmly setting me down the path of a career in Software Development.

I have finally found what I want to do as a career and I enjoy every minute of it. I love challenging myself and working within this field has presented me with no end of challenges, with new languages and problems presenting themselves around every corner. I enjoy learning and overcoming these problems to produce a product that I can be proud of.

QUB Libertarian Society App


Client: QUB Libertarian Society Date: June 2016 Service: Android App

The QUB Libertarian Society App began as a personal project that I undertook in order to become familiar with the new Ionic 2 framework. However, as I developed it, it became clear that the app could serve a functional purpose for the QUB Libertarian Society, which I established back in 2013, at various recruitment fairs, allowing students who didn't have the time to stop and talk to download the app, find out what the society was all about and sign up through the app itself.

Due to it being a personal project, the app in only currently availabe on Android devices.

Welcome to Boston App


Client: Irish International Immigrant Center, Boston Date: April 2016 Service: Cross-Platform App

Built using the Ionic Framework, the Welcome to Boston app was the first project I worked on at Aptcode Solutions. The purpose of the app was to give both Android and iOS users a new way of gaining access to information and services provided by the Irish International Immigration Center in Boston. I personally lead the development of the app from start to finish, from writing the initial proposal bid, to designing, coding, testing and releasing the app.

Below is a promotional video made for the app's release and was presented at the Irish International Immigrant Center's annual Business Leaders Breakfast on the 28th April 2016. It was produced by InterMedia Ireland, a partner company of Aptcode Soultions.
